# Glossary of Metrics

## Business Performance Metrics

**Net Sales**
Total revenue after discounts have been applied. Calculated as Gross Sales minus Total Discounts.

**Gross Sales**
Total revenue before any discounts are applied. The full price customers would pay without promotions.

**Total Orders**
The total number of completed transactions during a given period.

**Average Order Value (AOV)**
The average amount spent per order. Calculated as Total Sales divided by Total Orders.

**Median Ticket**
The middle value of all order amounts when sorted from lowest to highest. Less affected by extreme values than average.

**Year-over-Year (YoY) Growth**
The percentage change in a metric compared to the same period in the previous year. Shows growth or decline trends.

---

## Customer Metrics

**Unique Customers**
The total number of distinct customers who placed at least one order during a given period.

**New Customers**
Customers placing their first-ever order during a given period.

**Returning Customers**
Customers who have placed more than one order (also called "Repeating Customers").

**One-Timer**
A customer who has placed exactly one order and never returned.

**Customer Retention Rate**
The percentage of customers who return to place additional orders. Calculated as Repeating Customers divided by Total Customers.

**Churn Rate**
The percentage of customers who do not return after their first order. Calculated as 1 minus Retention Rate.

**Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)**
The total revenue generated by a customer across all their orders. Calculated as the sum of all order values for that customer.

**Average Orders per Customer**
The average number of orders placed by each customer. Calculated as Total Orders divided by Total Customers.

**Customer Lifetime (Avg Lifetime)**
The time period between a customer's first order and their most recent order, measured in months.

**First-Order Retention**
The percentage of customers who place a second order after their first. Key indicator of onboarding success.

---

## Retention Status Categories

**Active Customer**
A customer who placed an order within the last 30 days.

**Recent Customer**
A customer who placed an order 30-60 days ago. Still engaged but needs re-engagement.

**Sleeping Customer**
A customer who placed an order 60-180 days ago. At risk of becoming permanently inactive.

**Inactive Customer**
A customer who has not placed an order in more than 180 days. Likely churned permanently.

**Days Since Last Order**
The number of days between a customer's most recent order and the analysis date (December 31, 2024).

---

## Discount & Promotion Metrics

**Discount Rate**
The percentage of gross revenue given away as discounts. Calculated as Total Discounts divided by Gross Sales.

**Orders with Discount**
The number of orders where any discount (promo code, loyalty reward, etc.) was applied.

**Percentage of Discounted Orders**
The proportion of all orders that used a discount. Calculated as Orders with Discount divided by Total Orders.

**Average Discount per Order**
The average monetary value of discounts on orders that used promotions. Calculated as Total Discounts divided by Discounted Orders.

**Average Discount Percentage**
The average percentage off on discounted orders. Shows how deep discounts typically are.

**Revenue per € Discounted**
A measure of promotional efficiency. Calculated as Net Sales divided by Total Discounts. Higher values indicate better ROI.

**Promo Adoption Rate**
The percentage of eligible orders that used a specific promotion during its active period.

---

## Operational Metrics

**Shift**
Time-of-day grouping for orders:
- Morning: Before 4 PM
- Afternoon: 4 PM - 8 PM  
- Night: 8 PM and later

**Day Type**
Classification of orders:
- Weekday: Monday through Friday
- Weekend: Saturday and Sunday

**Seasonality**
Recurring patterns in sales based on time of year (e.g., summer slump, Q1 peak).

---

## Promo Types Referenced

**SXGY (Spend X Get Y)**
Threshold-based promotions where customers must spend a minimum amount to unlock a fixed discount.
- Example: S35G5 = Spend €35, Get €5 off
- Example: S40G10 = Spend €40, Get €10 off

**2x1 (Two-for-One / BOGO)**
Buy-one-get-one-free promotion. Customer orders two items but pays for only one.

**XX% OFF**
Percentage-based discount applied to the entire order total.
- Examples: 20% OFF, 25% OFF, 30% OFF

**Free Delivery**
Waived delivery fee, but no discount on food items.
